SLOTH

Description
Sloth is the choice of comfort and avoidance over action. It wastes potential, breaks commitments, and forces others to bear the consequences of one's inaction.

Punishment
Souls are trapped waist-deep in sticky mud, pulled backward by shadowy figures representing abandoned responsibilities. Glowing clocks above speed up whenever they hesitate, threatening to drag them under entirely.

Allegory
The mud and shadows symbolize the weight of excuses and ignored duties. The speeding clocks remind that time lost can never be regained, showing that delay only deepens one's downfall.

Reflection
These sins show how modern society struggles with procrastination and avoidance, often choosing comfort over responsibility even when it harms others. The circle of Sloth feels important to me because I relate to the pressure of time slipping away and the consequences of delaying important tasks. Through this version of the Inferno, I want readers to understand that wasted time cannot be reclaimed and that facing responsibilities today prevents heavier burdens tomorrow.
